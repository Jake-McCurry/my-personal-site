A little bit about me

I am currently based in Mossyrock, Washington, with my small growing family. After seven years in higher education my journey into tech started with a desire to create. I love working with my hands and the satisfaction that comes from a finished product, so as a people manager, I started a novice woodworking hobby. As I approached my third decade I began exploring careers that would provide creative hands-on work while supporting my family. When I found Skillcrush's Free Code Camp, I realized that learning to code wasn't as daunting as I imagined and even provided freedom and versatility.

I was hooked and immediately signed up to learn HTML, CSS, Git/Github, Javascript, and UX Design. In January 2020 I finished the HTML/CSS curriculum and moved on to Git/Github. Though education will always shape my worldview I am pumped to be working towards finished products again. After officially transitioning out of higher education I am glad to be a burgeoning learner equipped to use my organization skills and eye for detail to write clean, elegant code.

Hand-Coded HTML

My focus is writing clean, well-formatted, semantic HTML5 by hand to make sure that the content is easy to read, easy to collaborate, trouble-shoot and accessible.

Well-Organized CSS

I pride myself on writing CSS that is easy to read and build on. I focus on keeping my CSS lean and fast to load, and I make it a habit to stay up to date on current best practices.

Ease Converting Designs into Code

You can trust me to take a designer's PSD and quickly & accurately convert it into a webpage that is pixel-perfect match.
